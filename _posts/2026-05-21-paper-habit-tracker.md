---

I wanted a simple printable weekly habit tracker.

This turned out to be much harder to find than expected.

Most of the free templates I came across were either:

- overly complicated,
- built around app ecosystems,
- visually cluttered,
- or hidden behind paywalls and signup forms.

So I made two `.docx` templates instead.

They are intentionally minimal. Just a weekly layout with enough structure to keep track of a handful of habits without turning the process itself into a project.

To be clear, I am not anti productivity app. I use the iPhone Reminders app constantly and find it incredibly helpful for emptying my brain of tasks, errands, and random obligations before I forget them.

But habits feel different.

Tasks are irregular and reactive. Habits happen at defined intervals. They are repetitive by design. I wanted a way to keep track of them without opening the distraction rectangle every time.

There is also something nice about a physical sheet of paper sitting on a desk. It remains quietly visible throughout the day in a way that software does not.

Both versions are designed to be printed repeatedly and marked up by hand.

If you were also looking for a decent free printable habit tracker and somehow kept ending up on productivity SaaS landing pages, perhaps these will be useful.
