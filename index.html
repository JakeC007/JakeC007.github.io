Jake Chanenson

Childrens' privacy scholar. AI safety for youth. Law-informed HCI.

I'm a CS PhD student studying childrens' privacy and AI safety at the University of Chicago, where I work with Marshini Chetty at the Amyoli Internet Research Lab. My research asks what it actually takes to protect children and youth in digital environments, and as generative AI moves into classrooms, that question has become more urgent than ever. I bring a Master of Legal Studies from UChicago Law to the work, which means I can see both what the law currently protects and where the gaps are. Finishing my PhD in spring 2027. My work spans research, law, and policy, and I am drawn to roles across academia, industry, and government where that breadth is useful.

Research
Childrens' Privacy, AI & HCI
My work investigates how children and youth interact with AI and EdTech, what data is collected, what risks exist, and what it looks like to design systems that genuinely protect young users. Includes work on privacy policy automation with LLMs and a CHI 2026 workshop on developmentally safe generative AI.
