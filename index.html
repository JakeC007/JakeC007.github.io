Jake Chanenson

Usable privacy researcher. Kids & AI. A little bit of law.

I build research at the intersection of HCI, children's privacy, and AI — studying how kids interact with technology and what it means to design systems that are genuinely safe for them. I work with Marshini Chetty at the Amyoli Internet Research Lab, and I hold a Master of Legal Studies from UChicago Law with a focus on privacy and copyright law. I'm finishing my PhD in 2026 and am actively exploring both academic and industry research positions.

Children's Privacy, AI & HCI
My work investigates how kids interact with AI and EdTech — what data is collected, what risks exist, and what it looks like to design systems that genuinely protect young users. Includes work on privacy policy automation with LLMs and a CHI 2026 workshop on developmentally safe generative AI.
